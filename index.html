Technical breadth without losing the details.

My work sits at the intersection of infrastructure, software, data, and operations. I enjoy understanding how every layer fits together—from physical hardware and network paths to applications, automations, and the people using them.

I bring enterprise infrastructure experience, software and scripting skills, graduate-level data work, and a hands-on lab where ideas are built and tested for real.

That combination has taught me to approach technical problems from both directions: zoom out far enough to design the right system, then zoom in far enough to find the one configuration, dependency, or assumption causing trouble.

This site brings together my professional interests, technical projects, Master's-program case studies, and the self-hosted infrastructure behind sherga.net.
